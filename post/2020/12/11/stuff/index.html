Hard Tacos vs. Soft Tacos

December 11, 2020   

Why Not Both?

A little girl once said “porque no los dos”. With these legendary words, she broke the universal misconception that we as humans must choose between two good things; that we are eternally damned to giving up one thing in order to have the other. Hard tacos vs soft tacos… With that phrase that will be immortally etched into the fabric of time, we no longer have to choose between hard tacos and soft tacos. We can have both.

It is in this spirit of insatiable consumerism that I gladly introduce possibly the greatest combination of complementary items since hard and soft tacos… This transcendant duo is … R and Python, together at last!

A demonstration?

After hearing such a tantalizing story, one most certainly wants to see the goods in action. Similar to how “Old El Paso” created the means to have both types of taco for dinner, “Reticulate” gives us the power to utilize “Python” in R.

library(reticulate)
Old_El_Paso <- "Hard Tacos"

Now for the python code.

Reticulate="Soft Tacos"
print(r.Old_El_Paso,Reticulate)

## ('Hard Tacos', 'Soft Tacos')
